GRACE AT THE CROSSROADS

Finding God When the Road Runs Out

Devotional & Testimony

Day One — The Junction at Nsawam

I have stood at many junctions in my life, but none tested me like the one at
Nsawam. I was thirty-four, newly retrenched, with three children in school fees I
could no longer pay and a wife whose faith, unlike mine, had not yet cracked. That
morning I sat at the lorry station with exactly enough coins for one fare — either
to town to beg my former manager for my job back, or to the small church where a
prayer meeting had been announced. Pride pulled me one way. Something quieter
pulled me the other. This little book is the story of what happened when I finally
let the quieter voice win, and of the thirty days of grace that followed.

Perhaps you are standing at your own junction today. Perhaps the road you planned
has simply run out — a marriage that has gone cold, a business that has collapsed,
a diagnosis you did not expect, a prayer that seems to have gone unanswered for so
long you have stopped expecting a reply. If so, I want you to know two things
before we begin. First, that God is not absent from the crossroads; He is often
most present precisely where our own maps end. And second, that grace is rarely a
straight road — it is usually a series of small, trembling steps taken in the dark,
each one confirmed only after you have dared to move.

Over the next thirty days, I will not give you easy answers or a formula for
prosperity. I have grown suspicious of preachers who promise that faith is a
vending machine. Instead, I will walk with you through Scripture, through my own
failures, and through the testimonies of ordinary Ghanaians — market traders,
teachers, widows, students — who discovered that the end of their strength was the
beginning of His. Each day closes with a single verse to carry, one honest
question to sit with, and a short prayer you can whisper even when you have no
words of your own.

If your faith is strong today, this book will deepen it. If your faith is hanging
by a thread, come as you are — a thread is enough for God to hold. Turn the page,
and let us begin the journey home.
